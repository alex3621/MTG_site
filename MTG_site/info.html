More Info

This page contains more information regarding the card game Magic The Gathering. Including a brief history on the game, some rules on how to play the game, and also some tips and strategies that help you win!

History of the Game

Magic The Gathering is a collectible trading card game that was created by mathematician Richard Garfield and published by Wizards of the Coast back in 1993. It quickly blew up around the world ever since its inception and has since become one of the most popular games even up till this day. It contains rich stories, characters, interesting background, vivid world-building, and much more than just a plain old card game.

The origins of Magic the Gathering traces bcak to the 1990s, when Richard Garfield was approached by Peter Adkison, the founder of Wizards of the Coast, to create a new game. Garfield has had success as a game designer previously, so he accepted the challenge. What insued was a card game that enables players to build a deck from a pool of cards.

The first set of Magic The Gathering cards was released in 1993 and it was an instant success. Players loved the creativity that goes into playing and deck-building along with interesting lore and characters to go along with it. As the game grew in popularity, Wizards adopted the style of releasing expansion sets on a regular basis, even up until today!

Official rules

Magic the Gathering currently has multiple formats, meaning that the same cards can be used under different rule sets. Which is another reason why it became so popular. The most popular formats include: Standard, Modern, Commander, Pioneer, and Pauper. I will quickly go over the rule sets for standard as it is the official "tournament" format.


  1. Each player shuffles their deck and draws seven cards to start the game. Who goes first is determined by rolling a die
  2. Players' decks are entirely constructed by themselves, made up of 60 cards. These cards need to be from within the standard expansion sets
  3. Players can play one land card each turn. Land cards produce mana based on its type, mana includes: black, white, red, green, blue.
  4. Manas can be tapped to cast spells. Each spell has its own casting cost. Once cast, the spell appears on the battlefield
  5. Creature spells have abilities associted with them. They also have power and toughness values used in combat. The goal of the game is to get your opponent to 0 life using your own spells.

Tips and Strategies

  • Always play your land card each turn. This allows you to cast more powerful spells as the game goes on.
  • Spend all your mana every turn, mana is a valuable resource that can be turned into advantages.
  • Have a healthy balance of magical spells and creature spells as well as land cards in your deck.
  • Be patient and wait for your opportunity to strike!
